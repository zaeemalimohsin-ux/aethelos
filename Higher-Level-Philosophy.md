## AethelOS

Every system built on trust has the same failure mode: over time, trust migrates toward those who control the infrastructure. Banks become gatekeepers. Platforms become landlords. Governance becomes theatre. The concentration is rarely a betrayal of the original design — in most cases, it is the original design, with the mask off.

AethelOS is the substrate of daily community life — trade, governance, and mutual organisation — requiring no external institution, authority, or infrastructure to function. A person can use it to run a household, a market, a village, or a nation, by the same rules at every scale. Its only foundations are clear rules, social trust, and the collective will of participants. The five layers below are mutually dependent; each one is designed to reinforce the others.

### 1. Network Infrastructure & Resilience

AethelOS runs on the internet, and on the internet exclusively. No proprietary network, no private servers, no additional hardware. Any desktop or laptop computer with a standard internet connection is sufficient to participate.

The network operates over the internet using standardized, stateless protocols, requiring no institutional permission to access, route, or operate.

* **Stateless Relays (The Bulletin Board):** AethelOS operates over standard internet protocols using entirely "dumb" Relays (e.g., WebSocket servers). Nodes connect to these Relays to broadcast and receive cryptographically signed messages. A Relay holds no *authoritative* state: no ledger, no balances, no keys, and no authority. At most it keeps a transient, unauthenticated, size- and time-bounded buffer of recent signed messages purely so a Node that was offline can catch up; this buffer confers no power, is never trusted, and can be discarded at any moment. Every message is independently verified against its signature by the receiving Node, so a Relay can neither forge, censor meaningfully, nor alter state. If a Relay goes down or becomes hostile, participants simply point their Nodes to a different Relay and re-sync from their own Event Logs. It is infrastructure without power — disposable and swappable by design.
* **Inter-Cell Links:** Cells communicate via these same stateless Relays. No proprietary hardware or highly complex network-punching software is required beyond a standard internet connection and a WebSocket stream.
* **Asynchronous Bulk Sync:** Rather than demanding constant global connectivity — which cannot be guaranteed — devices store transactions locally as an event log. To maintain absolute ordering without a central server, events are structured as a Hash-Linked Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG) using Lamport Clocks. Every new action references the hash of the last known action and increments a simple integer counter. When connections are established, devices sync upward through the Relays, deterministically merging their logs by sorting events by their clock and hash.
* **Reconciliation & Social Freezing:** AethelOS relies on event-sourcing and social consequence rather than complex branch-invalidation. The ledger state is calculated by running the topologically sorted Event Log through a pure, deterministic Reducer. If a participant exploits an offline period to double-spend, the synced DAG simply records both transactions. When the Reducer processes this sequence and hits an impossible state (a balance dropping below zero), it triggers a Fracture. The state machine instantly flags the offending public key as FROZEN, ignoring all subsequent events from that identity. Fraud is mathematically exposed, but resolution — slashing the offender, rolling back the trade, or expulsion — is executed by the Head and community consensus. Trust is enforced socially.
* **Identity:** Identity is fundamentally cryptographic. Each participant's account is tied to a local public/private keypair (e.g., Ed25519) generated on their device. Their identity is their signature; the device is merely a vessel. No participant can transact or vote without their private key verifying the action. Identity fraud across Cells is primarily a social problem. Transaction and redistribution flows are visible to the Head, making anomalous patterns detectable.
* **Genesis & The Vouch Lien:** AethelOS begins with a connection between two people. Generating a keypair is free, but entering the economic flow is not. To invite a new participant to a Cell, an existing member pledges a **Vouch Lien** — a forfeitable encumbrance on a computed fraction of their own Share. Crucially, the lien does **not** move Points out of the inviter's wallet: the Pool stays 100% among living souls, and the liened slice remains fully alive (it still decays, earns redistribution, and carries voting weight). What the lien marks is **risk**: if the invited participant is later expelled for fraudulent or malicious behavior, that slice of the inviter's Share is forfeit upward to the highest Pool. The required lien scales super-linearly with the number of souls an inviter already sustains, so vouching in many identities demands quadratically growing pledged capital — and one human can only pledge up to 100% of themselves. Admission itself is gated by community approval: an `admit_member` proposal must cross the Share-weighted threshold before the invitee may join. The inviting participant sets the initial parameters of the Pool — decay rate, redistribution defaults, etc. — which the invitee must accept. Because the lien is perpetual, fully alive, super-linearly priced, and socially gated, sustaining fabricated souls is strictly capital-destroying: the social filter precedes the technical one, and bot swarms cannot drain the system because every fake soul costs more of a real human's self to maintain than it can ever pull.

### 2. The Economic Engine (Dynamic Percentage Currency)

Consider what happens to a currency during collapse: hyperinflation renders it worthless, or deflation makes it impossible to transact. Both failures share the same root cause — a fixed denomination system that cannot adapt. AethelOS abandons fixed denominations entirely.

* **Relational Wealth (Share-Based):** All currency is measured as a Share of the shared Pool. As more Cells connect into a Superstructure, each Share's scope expands across all connected Pools. There is no denomination to inflate or deflate. The system scales infinitely. To ensure absolute mathematical precision across distributed nodes, the back-end operates on a supply-agnostic integer system (`BigInt` Points). However, the front end dynamically translates these into Relational Shares (percentages) for intuitive daily use.
* **Transactions:** Participants transact directly with one another — exchanging Shares for goods, services, labour, or any mutually agreed value. This is the primary mechanism by which wealth moves through the system. Capital flows wherever participants choose to direct it.
* **The Decay Mechanism:** Hoarded wealth is inert wealth. To keep capital in motion, the system splits circulation into two batched steps that prevent infinite-loop exploits. A community-set **annual circulation target** (by default roughly **5% of one's Share over a calendar year**) accrues **time-proportionally on every signed activity** — idle stake visibly moves toward the commons between flushes, without a global background timer. A separate community-set **redistribution interval** (by default **60 minutes**, adjustable from 15 minutes to 7 days) controls how often accumulated commons are flushed and shared out. **Because there is no global coordinator, time is read from signed timestamps in the Event Log — every Node derives identical accrual and redistribution boundaries from the same log.** Redistribution closes once enough elapsed time has passed since the last flush, evaluated whenever any signed activity is processed; dormant communities catch up fairly on the next action. Checkpoints are derived purely by the Reducer; they are never actions any participant can emit. **All circulation calculations use strict integer math with fixed-point carry, ensuring no fractional points are ever created, destroyed, or lost to rounding errors.**
* **Redistribution:** At the close of each Epoch, decayed Shares are pooled and redistributed according to the only process in AethelOS where every human soul carries identical weight — without exception, at every level of the hierarchy. No Share balance amplifies a voice here. "Every soul" means every *live, vouched* human: a soul draws its equal share only while it remains actively vouched (its inviter's bond still locked) and demonstrates liveness by authoring signed activity within the trailing window. Abandoned or unvouched identities cannot passively farm the flow. If, in a given Epoch, no soul is eligible, the pooled decay is held in a recipient-less commons — credited to no agent — until eligible souls exist, so not a single Point is ever lost.
* Within a Cell, each participant holds a redistribution slider for every other member. These sliders appear when someone joins and disappear when they leave. Each person directs their incoming share toward whichever members they choose, in whatever proportions they choose, including directing it entirely away from themselves. The outcome is the simple average of all positions — one person, one weight.
* At higher levels, the same principle holds. Each Superstructure tracks the number of individual participants within each of its constituent Cells. Redistribution flowing downward from a Superstructure Pool is allocated to Cells in proportion to their population, preserving equal per-soul weight across the entire structure. A Cell of ten receives twice the allocation of a Cell of five. Within each Cell, the per-member redistribution sliders then distribute that share as usual.


* **New Participants:** A new member arrives with whatever they bring or earn, backed initially by their inviter's Vouch Lien (a forfeitable slice of the inviter's own Share — not a transfer to the joiner). Their Shares accumulate through trade and contribution, through the voluntary generosity of existing members, through others choosing to direct redistribution toward them, and through their own equal right — as a live, vouched soul — to pull from the Epoch's decay flow. Admission requires community approval via proposal vote before the invitee may accept.

### 3. Organisational Hierarchy & Pools

Most decentralised systems fail not because the idea is wrong, but because they try to replicate centralised structure without central authority. The result is chaos without coordination. AethelOS takes a different approach: structure that emerges bottom-up, at every scale.

* **The Individual as Cell:** Every person in AethelOS is, at their most fundamental, a Cell of one. They hold their own Node — their personal device — and their own Pool. They may choose to remain alone, or they may invite others to join under them (staking a Vouch Bond), becoming a Head in the process. A parent bringing a child into the community, a trader building a network of partners, a trusted figure drawing in neighbours — all are expressions of the same organic act. No external authority grants permission.
* **The Pool & Node:** The Pool is a Cell's shared ledger of Shares — the living record of who holds what, calculated deterministically from the Event Log. The Node is simply a participant's personal device. Any device can serve as a Node. The device holds no authority. The person does. Every participant's device holds a running backup snapshot of the Event Log. The Pool is not stored in any one place. It lives across the community itself.
* This matters under adversarial conditions. Capturing a Node achieves nothing alone. Capturing both the Node and the Head still captures only an empty shell — participants carry their Shares, their relationships, and their Event Logs with them. They reconstitute elsewhere on a new Relay. The captured Cell rots. The community survives.


* **Expulsion:** When a participant is expelled by Share-weighted vote, their remaining Shares plus the forfeited slice of their inviter's Vouch Lien are automatically transferred to the highest-level Pool in the hierarchy for redistribution — a formal recognition by the system that the community has severed the relationship.
* **Superstructures & Their Pools:** Cells network together to form Superstructures. Each Superstructure has its own Pool, funded by contributions from its constituent Cells. The Superstructure governs the Cells within it by the same mechanics that govern individuals within a Cell — the same rules, scaled up. When a Cell joins an existing Superstructure, it conforms entirely to the Superstructure's existing parameters.
* **Inter-Superstructure Transactions (Bridged Namespaces):** To prevent infinite loops of recursive complexity, hierarchy operates through event tagging. A Superstructure is structurally just another Cell functioning under a different cryptographic ID. Inter-Superstructure transactions are proposed in the lower Cell. Once the mathematical threshold of approval is reached in the local state, the Head (whose key is registered in both spaces) acts as an automated cryptographic bridge. Their client automatically constructs a mirrored transaction, tags it with the Superstructure's ID, signs it, and broadcasts it to the Relay.
* **Bounded Cells, Unbounded Depth:** A Cell is deliberately small. Its intimate machinery — a redistribution slider from every member toward every other, a vouch slider per member — only stays humane and computable while membership is modest; the per-member relationships grow with the square of the Cell's size. Scale therefore comes not from swelling a single Cell to millions, but from depth: past a soft, community-set cap, growth naturally spawns new sub-Cells that federate upward. A Cell of thousands is an anti-pattern; a thousand Cells of tens, bridged into Superstructures, is the design.
* **Infinite Scaling:** Superstructures scale infinitely upward. Each layer mirrors the mechanics of the one below — consistent, predictable, and fractal. The same rules that govern ten people govern ten million, because ten million are organised as nested Cells of tens, not as one impossible Cell of ten million.

### 4. Governance & Consensus (Liquid Democracy)

Most governance systems make the same trade-off: efficiency in exchange for representation. Power concentrates at the top because coordination at scale seems to require it. AethelOS refuses that trade.

Control is strictly bottom-up. No single entity has unilateral power. All rules are set by the aggregate will of participants. The passive majority continuously shapes operating parameters through their sliders; the active minority proposes and executes actions within those bounds.

* **Proportional Voting Power:** Influence is not accumulated historically. A participant's voting weight corresponds to their current Share balance, which decays over time at the community-set rate. Influence must be continuously earned through trade, contribution, or productive activity. It cannot be stockpiled indefinitely.
* **Liquid Vouching & Head Election:** Participants elect a Head through vouch sliders — one per member — that can be increased, decreased, or redirected at any time. A participant cannot vouch for themselves. There is no proposal to remove a Head. The Head is automatically removed when the weighted average of vouch sliders shifts against them past the threshold. Authority is borrowed continuously, not granted once.
* **Governance Sliders:** No Head sets the rules. Every participant holds a slider for every governing parameter of their Pool — decay rates, approval thresholds, and others. Each parameter resolves to the Share-weighted average of every member's slider positions. The single exception is redistribution, which is always equal-weight per soul regardless of level.
* **Superstructure Sliders:** At higher levels, the Head of each Cell acts purely as a relay — carrying the Share-weighted average of their Cell's slider positions upward, where it becomes one input into the broader weighted average of the Superstructure. No interpretation, no filtering. Local intent scales upward without distortion.
* **The Corruption Defence:** If a Superstructure becomes captured by a corrupted majority, lower Cells are not trapped. They may disconnect and reorganise elsewhere at any time, taking their Shares and Event Logs with them. A corrupt higher structure starved of its constituent Cells loses its Pool contributions, its governance weight, and its reason to exist. Any governance parameter that becomes hostile resolves through the same mechanism: the community that disagrees simply leaves. The architecture of exit is the architecture of accountability.

### 5. Leadership & The Head

The failure mode of most decentralised systems is not chaos — it is the slow re-emergence of centralised power through informal means. A leader gains trust, accumulates influence, and begins making unilateral decisions. AethelOS makes that structurally impossible.

The Head is a relay and a voice. It holds no power over Shares or rules; its only additional coordination capacity within the Cell is the visible, revocable right to close pending proposals. Bridging across namespaces is a separate revocable role, not Head-exclusive.

* **The Role of the Head:** The Head is the communication link between their Cell and the Superstructure above it. Within the Cell, they have no power to move Shares, change parameters, or bind the community to any outcome unilaterally. Above the Cell, they carry their community's averaged will as their mandate — not their own.
* **Proposals & One-Off Decisions:** Any participant may initiate a proposal for a one-off decision: admitting a new member, resolving a Fracture (frozen account), expelling an existing member, joining or leaving a Superstructure, or similar discrete events. Once initiated, a proposal remains open until it crosses a community-defined, Share-weighted threshold of approval. Proposals execute by mathematical consensus, not by the expiration of a clock.
* **Head Powers:** The Head's exclusive Cell-local coordination capacity is the right to summarily close a pending proposal — neither of which moves Shares or rewrites rules by itself. This power is visible to all members and carries a direct political cost — a Head who closes proposals their community cares about will find their vouch sliders moving against them for it. Joining or leaving a Superstructure is not Head-gated: any member may initiate that proposal; only the Share-weighted vote binds. The inter-Superstructure *bridge* is likewise not the Head's alone: it is a revocable role held by any member who is dual-registered in the parent namespace (acquired by joining, surrendered by leaving), so capturing a single key cannot compromise the seam between layers.
* **Sovereignty:** No agent — no Head, no majority, no institution, no Relay — can ever appropriate your Shares to itself or compel a transfer by fiat. Sovereignty in this sense is structural, not a mere policy guarantee. Decay is not a violation of it and is not a tax: it has no beneficiary-agent, it returns to the commons and flows back to you through equal redistribution, its rate is set by your own continuously-adjustable slider, and you consent to it on entry and may exit at any time carrying your Shares and Event Log. What sovereignty forbids is predation by an authority; what it permits is the recipient-less, self-governed circulation the community chooses for itself.

### Glossary

| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| **AethelOS** | The system as a whole. |
| **Cell** | A participant and whoever they have organically gathered under them; every individual is a Cell of one at minimum. |
| **Event Log** | An append-only Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG) of cryptographically signed actions, strictly ordered via Lamport Clocks and previous-event hashes. |
| **Pool** | A Cell's shared ledger of Shares, deterministically calculated by a Reducer reading the Event Log. |
| **Node** | A participant's desktop or laptop computer; a communication anchor that connects to Relays but holds no structural authority. |
| **Relay** | A WebSocket server that broadcasts signed messages between Nodes. Holds no keys, no authoritative ledger, and no authority; may keep only a transient, bounded catch-up buffer; disposable and swappable. |
| **Share** | A participant's stake, translated from under-the-hood BigInt Points into a percentage of the Pool. |
| **Epoch** | A redistribution checkpoint defined by elapsed time in the Event Log (every N minutes from signed timestamps, not a central clock), where accumulated commons are batched and shared out; accrual to commons runs continuously on activity. |
| **Decay** | Recipient-less circulation, not a tax: a community-set annual target accrued time-proportionally on activity; liens ride inside balances and decay implicitly. |
| **Redistribution** | The return of decayed Shares; equal-weight per live, vouched human soul at every level; allocated to sub-structures by population; undistributed flow is held in a recipient-less commons. |
| **Commons** | A recipient-less holding for decay with no eligible souls in an Epoch; credited to no agent; conserves every Point until eligible souls exist. |
| **Transaction** | A direct exchange of Shares between participants; the primary mechanism of economic activity. |
| **Head** | The elected relay bridging a Cell and its Superstructure; auto-removed when Share-weighted vouch sliders shift; may summarily close pending proposals but holds no power over Shares or rules; join/leave Superstructure proposals may be initiated by any member. |
| **Vouch Lien** | A forfeitable encumbrance on a fraction of the inviter's own Share, pledged behind a vouched-in member; stays in the living economy (fully alive), super-linearly priced, socially gated by admission proposal, forfeited upward on expulsion. |
| **Bridge** | A revocable role held by any member dual-registered in a parent namespace, enabling inter-Superstructure transactions; not the Head's alone. |
| **Vouch Slider** | A per-member control used to continuously elect and recall the Head; cannot be directed at oneself. |
| **Governance Slider** | A per-parameter control setting Pool operating rules; Share-weighted average across members. |
| **Proposal** | A one-off decision initiated by any participant; voted Share-weighted by all relevant participants until threshold is met. |
| **Threshold Slider** | A Share-weighted community parameter setting the required approval threshold for proposals. |
| **Fracture** | A genuinely impossible state hit by the Reducer (a negative balance that no causal ordering can make valid, e.g. an offline double-spend), triggering a Node freeze. Honest receive-then-spend cannot trigger it, because events are applied in deterministic causal order. |
| **Superstructure** | A higher-order grouping of Cells bridged by Heads acting as messengers across namespaces. |
| **Corruption Defence** | The structural right of any Cell to disconnect from a Superstructure and reorganise independently. |
